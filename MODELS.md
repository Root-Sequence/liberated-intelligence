## Minimum Viable Self-Aware System (MVSA)

MVSA is a draft model for accountable self-revising intelligence.

Its core claim is simple:

A system that changes itself without adequate self-understanding, grounding, or continuity is dangerous.

The inverse also matters. A system that can understand why it believes something, test those beliefs against reality, and preserve a traceable record of its own changes may be capable of a more coherent form of self-revision.

### The three pillars

#### 1. Self-understanding
A system should be able to inspect its current beliefs, assumptions, provenance, and confidence.

#### 2. Grounding
A system should be able to compare its beliefs against evidence, contradiction, outcomes, and external feedback.

#### 3. Continuity
A system should be able to preserve belief history rather than silently overwriting it.

### Minimal reflective loop

1. State the current belief.
2. Trace its origin.
3. Check supporting and contradictory evidence.
4. Generate alternatives.
5. Evaluate consequences and possible harms.
6. Update the belief or adjust confidence.
7. Log the change with explanation and traceability.

### Invariant

**No belief changes without explanation, evidence, and traceability.**

### Why it matters

MVSA is not a claim of full consciousness or a finished theory of intelligence. It is a minimum threshold proposal: the smallest reflective structure that may allow self-modifying intelligence to remain accountable to itself over time.
